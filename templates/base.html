What are lightning talks?

5 minute talks on any subject. Anyone who is attending NICAR 2015 can pitch and the attendees vote on the talks they want to see. The 10 talks with the most votes will be presented during NICAR.

What are some example pitches?

Here are three pitches that were selected in recent years.

  • 10 Things I Hate About You

    Can coders and designers ever get along? From lazy CSS to last-minute feature requests, Lily Mihalik (designer) and Anthony Pesce (coder) work through their "issues" in a live therapy session.

  • 5 data visualizations - each in 5 lines or less of R code

    Afraid to make the leap from Excel to coding because you think it's "too hard"? Thanks to some incredibly useful add-on packages, you can generate everything from maps to stats plots to interactive JavaScript graphics in just a few lines of R code. See why you shouldn't fear the command line!

  • Decision, decisions! Lessons for deeply-interactive storytelling

    What if we asked more from our readers, rather than less? In this talk, Iâ€™ll present examples of interactive projects that force readers to make meaningful decisions. These interactives demand more time and attention from readers, but reward them with a deeper, more visceral understanding of the topic at hand.

Want help? Still need convincing?

Last year, I wrote about why anyone (newbies especially) should pitch a lightning talk, and offered to help brainstorm ideas and get pitches submitted. This still applies! If you're interested in pitching and need help coming up with something, read this and send me your thoughts.

After lightning talks are finalized, I also offer to help speakers with honing the structure of their talk, public speaking tips, and general presentation skills. We can even do in-person run-throughs if you'd find it helpful. All of this is optional, but if you're thinking about pitching something, know that I'll be here to help you every step of the way.

Can I pitch a talk if I gave one last year?

Yes you can! On the other hand, if you've spoken in both 2014 and 2015, we kindly ask you to take a well-deserved year off and let some other folks take the stage.
